1NVIDIA       2Georgia Tech       3University of Washington

Overview



HITL-TAMP decomposes a robot manipulation task (such as making coffee) into planning-based and learning-based control segments. The planning-based segments are handled by a TAMP planner and the learning-based segments are handled by a human demonstrator (during data collection) and a policy trained on demonstrations (during policy deployment).


Contributions

  1. We introduce HITL-TAMP, an efficient data-collection system for long-horizon and contact-rich manipulation tasks that synergistically combines and trades off control between a TAMP system and a human teleoperator.

  2. HITL-TAMP contains novel components including (1) a mechanism that allows TAMP to learn planning conditions from a small number of demonstrations and (2) a queueing system that allows an operator to manage a fleet of parallel data collection sessions.

  3. We conduct a user study across 15 users to compare HITL-TAMP with a conventional teleoperation system. Users collectively gathered over 1.4K demos, more than 3x the conventional teleoperation system, given the same time budget. Proficient agents (over 75% success) could be trained from just 10 minutes of non-expert teleoperation data.

  4. We collected an additional 2.1K demos with HITL-TAMP across 12 contact-rich and long-horizon tasks and show that HITL-TAMP often produces near-perfect agents. This took a single operator a few hours, as opposed to operator teams and days needed in prior work.
